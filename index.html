✦ My Story

Art rooted in tradition, bloomed by passion

From the narrow lanes of Unn Patiya, Surat, Sana has been adorning hands since childhood — learning the sacred art of mehndi from her grandmother, who believed every swirl carries a blessing.

Her designs are a dialogue between Arabic geometry, Rajasthani florals, and modern fusion — each piece hand-crafted with natural henna paste, leaving a deep, long-lasting stain and an unforgettable impression.

✦ The Experience

A journey from vision
to lasting colour

01

Design Consultation

We begin with a friendly chat via WhatsApp or call. You share your event details, dress styling, and any inspiration photos. Together, we curate a unique design style tailored just for you.

02

Fresh Henna Preparation

24 hours before your booking, I personally prepare 100% natural, chemical-free henna paste using premium essential oils. This ensures the safest application and the richest, darkest stain.

03

The Application Art

I arrive at your location (or you visit my studio). We set up a comfortable space, and I begin freehand drawing your design. It's a relaxing process where your skin becomes a canvas.

04

Aftercare & Bloom

Once dried, I provide a homemade lemon-sugar sealant. Keep the paste on for 6-8 hours. As you scrape it off, the color will naturally oxidize into a deep mahogany red over the next 48 hours.

✦ Common Queries

Everything you need
to know

Do you use chemical or "fast-color" cones? +
Absolutely not. I strictly use 100% organic, natural henna powder mixed with safe essential oils (like eucalyptus or lavender) and lemon juice. Chemical cones can burn the skin and leave fake colors. My natural paste is safe for all skin types and yields a beautiful, authentic dark stain.
Do you provide home-visit services? +
Yes! While I have a studio space in Unn Patiya, Surat, I frequently travel to clients' homes and event venues across Surat for Bridal and Group bookings. Travel charges may apply depending on the exact distance.
How far in advance should I book my bridal mehndi? +
For the wedding season (November to February), I recommend booking at least 2-3 months in advance to secure your dates. For non-bridal or festive mehndi, 1-2 weeks prior notice is usually sufficient.
How can I get the darkest stain possible? +
Leave the paste on for at least 6-8 hours (overnight is best). Avoid washing your hands with water/soap for 12 hours after scraping the crust off. Apply eucalyptus oil or Vicks vapor rub, and keep your hands warm. The stain will reach its peak darkness within 48 hours.
